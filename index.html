About The Game

In Minecraft, players explore a blocky, pixelated procedurally generated, three-dimensional world with virtually infinite terrain. Players can discover and extract raw materials, craft tools and items, and build structures, earthworks, and machines.
Minecraft has been rated as suitable for 7+ up to 13+ depending on which version of the game you are playing. Children aren't asked for proof of age when they sign up.

The game was created by Markus "Notch" Persson in the Java programming language. Following several early private testing versions, it was first made public in May 2009 before being fully released on November 18, 2011, with Notch stepping down and Jens "Jeb" Bergensten taking over development.

Creeper. Creepers are common hostile mobs in the overworld that spawn at night and in low-light areas. Unlike zombies, they don't get damaged by sunlight
A skeleton is an undead hostile mob that performs ranged attacks with a bow. A skeleton may spawn above a solid block at a light level of 0 from all Overworld except in mushroom fields and deep dark biomes, usually in small groups of up to four skeletons in Java Edition or two skeletons in Bedrock Edition.
Zombies are a Hostile Mob that spawn at night or in unlit areas and will burn to death in sunlight. They are the easiest mob to deal with since they do not have special abilities and move quite slowly. They look akin to the default player mode
